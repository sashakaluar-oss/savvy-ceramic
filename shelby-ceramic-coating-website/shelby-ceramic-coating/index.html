Common Questions

Ceramic Coating FAQs

Everything you need to know before booking.

What is ceramic coating and how does it work? +
Ceramic coating is a liquid polymer — a semi-permanent protective layer — that bonds chemically to your vehicle's factory paint. Unlike wax or sealant, it doesn't sit on top of the paint; it becomes part of it. The result is a hard, hydrophobic surface that repels water, dirt, UV rays, bird droppings, tree sap, and environmental contaminants. Once cured (24–48 hrs), it's extremely durable.
How long does ceramic coating last in Melbourne? +
Melbourne's climate — intense UV summers, acidic rain, and variable humidity — accelerates paint degradation. With a professional Shelby ceramic coating, most daily drivers can expect 3–5 years of protection. Garage-kept or weekend cars often see 6–7+ years. Consumer-grade DIY kits typically last 6–12 months, which is why professional application matters.
Is paint correction included or is it extra? +
It depends on your car's condition. Our Essential package suits newer cars with minimal imperfections. Our Premium and Ultimate packages include paint correction. Why does this matter? Ceramic coating amplifies whatever is underneath it — coating over swirl marks locks them in permanently. We'll inspect your paint honestly and tell you exactly what's needed.
How long does the job take? +
A basic ceramic coat with no paint correction typically takes 4–6 hours. If paint correction is required, allow 1–2 days. After coating, your car needs a curing period of 24–48 hours before it gets wet, so plan accordingly. We'll give you a clear timeline when you get your quote.
Is ceramic coating worth it for a daily driver? +
Absolutely. Daily drivers cop the most punishment — UV, bird droppings, tree sap, car park swirls, washing scratches. A ceramic coating means your daily driver stays cleaner, washes easier, and holds its value much better. Many of our customers find they need fewer washes because contaminants simply don't bond to the surface.
What's the difference between ceramic coating and paint protection film (PPF)? +
Paint Protection Film (PPF) is a physical barrier — a thick, clear film that absorbs rock chips and physical impacts. Ceramic coating is a chemical barrier that provides hydrophobic and UV protection but won't stop stone chips. Many premium car owners combine both: PPF on high-impact areas (bonnet, front bumper), ceramic coating over the rest. Ask Savvy which is right for your situation.
