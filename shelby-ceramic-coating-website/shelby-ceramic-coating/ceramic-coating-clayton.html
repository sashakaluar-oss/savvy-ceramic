What Is Ceramic Coating — and Why Clayton Drivers Choose It

Ceramic coating is a liquid polymer that bonds chemically to your vehicle's factory paint, forming a hard, semi-permanent protective layer. Unlike wax or sealant that sit on top of your paint and wash off within months, a professional ceramic coating becomes part of your paint.

For Clayton drivers, Melbourne's UV intensity, acidic rain, road grime on Princes Highway and Dandenong Road, and summer heat all accelerate paint degradation. A Shelby ceramic coat protects against all of it — while making your car easier to clean and significantly better-looking.

What it protects against: UV rays and oxidation, bird droppings and tree sap, brake dust and industrial fallout, water spots and acid rain, fine swirl marks from improper washing, environmental contaminants.

How long does it last? With a professional application, expect 3–5 years on a daily driver and 5–7+ years on a garage-kept vehicle. Consumer-grade DIY kits typically last under 12 months.

Does My Car Need Paint Correction First?

This is the most important question before any ceramic coat. Ceramic coating amplifies what's underneath it — a perfect finish becomes more perfect, but swirl marks and scratches also get locked in permanently. We inspect every car honestly and tell you exactly what's needed. Our Premium and Ultimate packages include paint correction.

Is ceramic coating worth it for a daily driver? Absolutely. Daily drivers cop the most punishment from UV, bird droppings, tree sap, and wash-induced swirls. Ceramic coating means your car stays cleaner, washes easier, and holds resale value significantly better than an unprotected car.

Clayton Ceramic Coating FAQ

Do you come to Clayton or do I need to bring my car to you? +
We operate from our detailing studio in Clayton. Ceramic coating requires a controlled environment free from dust and wind to ensure a flawless, streak-free application. Call Savvy on 0490 699 614 to arrange drop-off and collection.
Can I wash my car right after ceramic coating? +
No — ceramic coating requires 24–48 hours to cure fully before contact with water. We'll give you detailed aftercare instructions. After curing, you can wash your car as normal — you'll immediately notice how much easier it is to clean.
Will ceramic coating protect against stone chips? +
Ceramic coating adds hardness to your paint surface and helps resist minor surface scratches, but it won't stop stone chips from high-speed road debris. For stone chip protection on Clayton's busier roads (Princes Highway, Dandenong Road), we recommend combining ceramic coating with Paint Protection Film (PPF) on the front bumper and bonnet.
