Why New Car Paint Needs Protection Immediately

Most people assume a brand-new car's paint is indestructible. It's not. Clear coat is soft and vulnerable from the moment it leaves the factory. In the time between the factory and your driveway, your car has likely already been washed multiple times, transported, detailed by the dealer, and sat in outdoor storage — all opportunities for fine scratches and swirl marks to form.

The moment you drive your new car home, it's exposed to Melbourne's UV, bird droppings, tree sap, car park scrapes, and road grime. Without protection, these elements start degrading your paint within months.

Why the Day You Drive Off the Lot Is the Best Time

FAQ

New Car Paint Protection FAQs

How soon after buying a new car should I get ceramic coating? +
As soon as possible — ideally within the first week. The longer you wait, the more likely the paint is to pick up swirl marks from routine washing, parking lot brushes, and environmental contamination. We can usually book new car clients within a few days. Call Savvy on 0490 699 614.
Does ceramic coating void my new car warranty? +
No. Ceramic coating is applied over the top of your factory paint and does not affect the mechanical warranty. It is purely a protective surface treatment. Many manufacturer warranties are actually enhanced by ceramic coating since it prevents the paint damage that would otherwise void cosmetic warranty claims.
My dealer offered paint protection for $1,800 — should I take it? +
Ask specifically what product is being applied and whether it's a true ceramic coating or a paint sealant. Most dealer packages are paint sealants (not true ceramic) with a significant margin built in. For $1,800 from a dealer, you should be getting professional ceramic coating with a multi-year guarantee. Compare that to Shelby's transparent pricing and Work Guarantee before deciding.
